# FinTrack: Purpose and Problem-Solving Framework

## The Problem
Financial instability often stems from three core issues:
1. **Lack of Visibility:** Money "disappears" because transactions aren't tracked in real-time.
2. **Behavioral Friction:** Traditional budgeting is boring, leading to abandoned habits.
3. **Information Overload:** Users have data but don't know how to act on it (e.g., "Is ₱500 on coffee too much?").

## The FinTrack Solution

### 1. Habit Formation through Gamification (F-12 to F-15)
FinTrack solves the "boring" problem by turning finance into a game.
*   **Streaks & Points:** Rewards daily logging to build consistent habits.
*   **Badges:** Provides micro-goals and dopamine hits for financial milestones.
*   **Challenges:** Encourages specific positive behaviors (e.g., "No-spend weekends").

### 2. Radical Transparency & Clarity (F-07, F-09)
Raw numbers are hard to digest. FinTrack uses visual logic:
*   **50/30/20 Breakdown:** Immediately shows if a user is living beyond their means.
*   **Spending Charts:** Identifies "leakage" categories (Needs vs. Wants) at a glance.

### 3. Proactive Risk Management (F-10, F-11, F-16)
The system acts as a financial guardrail:
*   **Budget Warnings:** Alerts at 80% usage to prevent overspending *before* it happens.
*   **Savings Runway:** Translates balances into "days of survival," making the impact of spending tangible.
*   **Spike Detection:** Automatically flags unusual behavior relative to a 3-month average.

### 4. Intelligent Guidance (F-18)
**Minto AI** solves the "What do I do now?" problem. By using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), it analyzes real user data to provide specific, non-generic advice, acting as a 24/7 financial coach.

## Summary
FinTrack moves beyond simple record-keeping. It is a **behavioral engine** designed to convert passive users into active, disciplined managers of their own wealth.
