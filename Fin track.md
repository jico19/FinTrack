**FinTrack** is a personal money tracker for people who want to know where their money goes. Users write down what they earn and spend each day, sort it into categories, and see a clear picture of their finances on one page.

To make it more fun, FinTrack has a rewards system where users earn points for logging money regularly, collect badges for reaching goals, and keep a daily streak going — making it feel good to stay on top of your budget.

**1\. Accounts**

* **F-01 Sign up & log in** — Users create an account with a username, email, and password. They can log in and out with no extra steps like email confirmation.  
* **F-02 Profile page** — Each user has their own page showing their photo, a short bio, their current points, the badges they've collected, how many days in a row they've been logging, and a quick look at this month's money summary.

**2\. Recording Money**

* **F-03 Add a record** — Users can write down money they received or spent by filling in the amount, a short note, the date, and what type of spending it was. It shows up on the dashboard right away.  
* **F-04 Edit or remove a record** — Users can change or delete any record they made. The dashboard updates on its own after any change.  
* **F-05 View all records** — A list of all past records, shown newest first. Users can narrow it down by category, type (money in or money out), or date.  
* **F-06 Spending categories** — A fixed list of categories like Food, Rent, or Entertainment. Each one is already labeled as either a Need, a Want, or a Saving — this is what makes the budget breakdown work.


**3\. Dashboard**

* **F-07 Money overview** — The first page users see after logging in. Shows total money in, total money out, and what's left for the month. Also shows a simple chart of spending by category.  
* **F-08 Set a monthly limit** — Users can set a spending limit for each category. A progress bar shows how close they are to hitting that limit.

**4\. Budget Tools**

* **F-09 Needs / Wants / Savings breakdown** — Shows what percentage of spending this month went to Needs, Wants, and Savings. Each one gets a color to show if the user is doing well, spending too much, or not saving enough.  
* **F-10 How long will money last** — Looks at how much the user is spending per day this month and estimates how many days their savings will last at that rate. Shows a warning if the answer is under 30 days.  
* **F-11 Unusual spending warning** — If a user spends more than double their usual amount in any category this month compared to the last 3 months, a warning shows up on the dashboard.

**5\. Rewards**

* ay a user adds at least one record, their streak goes up by one. Missing a day resets it to zero. Both the current streak and their all-time best are shown on their profile.  
* **F-14 Badges** — Users earn badges automatically when they reach certain goals, like adding their first record, keeping a 7-day streak, or staying within their budget for a whole month.  
* **F-15 Weekly challenges** — Each week, a small challenge is given to the user, like spending nothing over the weekend or keeping food costs under a set amount. Finishing it gives bonus points and a badge.

**6\. Alerts**

* **F-16 Budget warning** — When a user reaches 80% of their spending limit in any category, a warning shows up on the dashboard so they know before they go over.  
* **F-17 Weekly recap** — Every Monday, a short summary appears on the dashboard showing last week's total spending, the category they spent most on, their streak, and any new badges they earned.
